Online Education

Using Data Analytics to Understand Stop-Out Rates

The Problem

Most online courses suffer from >70% drop-off rates, which ultimately leads to sub-par learning outcomes and skills development. We used data on xx students across >10 courses on MITx and Harvardx to understand potential factors driving this behaviour.

Certified: 0.12

Non-Certified: 0.02

No. of Forum Posts

Average no. of forum posts varies significantly across certified and non-certified segments, perhaps because they're a good measure of overall engagement level of the student.

MITx: 3.6%

Harvardx: 3.5%

Institute

Harvadx courses show a significantly higher completion rate, perhaps due to the nature of courses offered. MITx tends to have more technical courses, which are tougher to learn virtually.

Female: 4.2%

Male: 3.4%

Gender

Women show a completion rate 23% higher than men and 17% higher than the overall completion rate of 3.6%.

Highest: Spain, Poland, Russia

Lowest: Morocco, Bangladesh, Japan

Countries

There is significant variation in completion rates across countries. Countries with highest overall enrolment i.e., India and USA show relatively low completion rates of 4% and 2.8% respectively.

Highest Rate: Master's

Highest Enrolment: Doctorate, Less than secondary

Level of Education

Students with a Master's degree had the highest certification rate of 4.9%. Surprisngly, students with highest education of less than secondary also had a high certification rate of 4.2%.

Highest Enrolment: Spring (245K)

Highest Certification: Fall (4%)

Semester

Fall courses see highest completion in general, especially for Harvardx. Overall, Spring courses saw highest enrolment across genders and institutes.

Why does this matter?

Online courses are increasingly seen as a critical solution for the emerging skills gap globally, especially in developing countries. Although providers such as Udacity have shown to improve job prospects for students, it is imperative to understand causes for stop-out rates in online courses and assess potential solutions for the same. We hope this project will help initiate a continued in this direction.

Potential Solutions based on Analysis

Below, we outline a few solutions aimed at improving certification rates to explore further.

Improve engagement features (e.g., personalized messages, virtual mentorship) for courses aimed at college students and recent grads, especially in spring. Increase courses aimed at students with high school or lower highest level of education, especially during summer.

Assess course-specific attributes of top-performing courses such as ER22x and courses with high enrolments but low certification rates (e.g., 6.00x and CS50x).

Understand drivers of stop-out rates specific to males since they form 67% of the enrolment, but have a certification rate that is 24% lower than that for females.
